# Code of Conduct

Bloom is built for collaborative robotics work. We want the project to stay
welcoming, practical, and safe for people with different backgrounds in web,
robotics, research, design, and operations.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and constructive.
- Assume good intent, and clarify before escalating.
- Keep technical feedback focused on the work, not the person.
- Make space for junior contributors and non-web specialists.
- Remember that robot-facing software can affect physical systems; discuss
  safety concerns clearly and early.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Dismissing safety, security, or accessibility concerns without review.
- Deliberately disrupting discussions, reviews, or project work.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that makes collaboration unsafe or unwelcoming, contact a
repository maintainer. If GitHub content reporting is enabled for the
organization, you may also use GitHub's report flow.

Maintainers should review reports with care, protect people from retaliation,
and choose a proportionate response such as a reminder, moderation, removal of
content, or blocking a contributor from the project.
